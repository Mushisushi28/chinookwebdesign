Edmonton Small Business Web Design: What to Look For

Posted by Chinook Web Design | Last updated April 2026

If you're searching for web design services in Edmonton, you're in luck—there's no shortage of options. But not all web designers are created equal. Some specialize in massive corporate projects. Others churn out cheap templated sites with minimal support. You need someone who understands your needs and can deliver a website that actually works for your business.

Let me walk you through what to look for when choosing a web designer in Edmonton.

1. They Have a Portfolio of Small Business Work

What This Means

Look at their past work. Do they have examples of websites they've built for local Edmonton businesses? Can you see actual completed projects, not just design mockups? A good designer should have a portfolio showing real sites they've launched.

Pay special attention to businesses similar to yours. If you run a plumbing company, finding examples of HVAC or electrical contractor websites is relevant. If you own a restaurant, look for other restaurant and retail portfolios. A designer with proven experience in your industry understands your challenges.

2. They Explain Things in Plain English (Not Tech Jargon)

What This Means

A good web designer speaks your language. When you ask a question, they explain it in a way that makes sense—not with acronyms and technical babble that leaves you confused.

If someone starts throwing around terms like "semantic HTML," "meta tags," and "REST APIs" without explaining them first, they might be more interested in sounding smart than helping you. You deserve a designer who takes time to communicate clearly about what they're building and why.

3. They Ask Questions Before Quoting a Price

What This Means

Be wary of a designer who gives you a price on the first phone call. Every business is different. A good designer asks about:

  • Your business goals (what do you want the website to do?)
  • Your target customers (who are you trying to reach?)
  • Your budget and timeline
  • What features you actually need
  • How you'll measure success

A designer who understands your unique situation will give you a custom quote and strategy—not a one-size-fits-all package.

4. They Focus on Results, Not Just Pretty Design

What This Means

A beautiful website that doesn't bring in customers is just expensive decoration. A good designer in Edmonton understands that your website needs to work. It should:

  • Show up in Google search results for your industry
  • Be easy for customers to navigate
  • Load fast on phones and computers
  • Have clear calls-to-action (contact buttons, booking forms, etc.)
  • Convert visitors into leads or sales

When you ask "How will this help my business?" they should have concrete answers about lead generation, customer engagement, or sales—not just talk about aesthetics.

5. They Handle SEO and Local Search

What This Means

A website that doesn't show up in Google is invisible. A good Edmonton web designer understands local SEO—the practices that help your business appear when customers search for your type of service in your area.

They should discuss:

  • Keyword research (what are people actually searching for?)
  • On-page optimization (making your site search-engine friendly)
  • Local listings and Google Business Profile optimization
  • Mobile-first design (most searches happen on phones)
  • Site speed and technical health

If they don't bring up SEO, that's a red flag.

6. They Provide Ongoing Support

What This Means

Building a website isn't a one-time project. Your site needs regular updates, security patches, and maintenance. A good designer doesn't hand off the project and disappear.

Ask about:

  • What happens after launch?
  • Do they offer maintenance packages?
  • How quickly can they respond if something breaks?
  • Can they help you update content (photos, text, blog posts)?
  • What's included vs. what costs extra?

7. They're Local (Or at Least Responsive)

What This Means

Working with a local Edmonton designer has advantages: they understand the local market, they're in the same timezone, and you can meet in person if needed. But an out-of-town designer can work just fine if they're responsive and professional.

What matters most: they respond to emails/calls promptly, they're available when you need them, and they take pride in local client success. Some of the best Edmonton-based web designers are also building sites for clients across Canada—the key is their level of responsiveness and commitment.

Red Flags to Watch Out For

Too Cheap

If someone quotes $200 for a website, run. You get what you pay for. A quality small business website should cost at least several hundred dollars. If it costs less, it's likely a template with minimal customization and no real strategy.

Overly Pushy or Vague

A designer who pressures you to sign a contract immediately or won't answer questions clearly is not someone you want managing your online presence. Trust your gut.

No Examples or References

If they can't show you past work or don't have references, that's a warning. A reputable designer should be proud to show what they've built.

Promises Guaranteed Rankings

Anyone who promises you'll rank #1 on Google is lying. SEO takes time and effort. A good designer will explain what's realistic and set proper expectations.

Poor Communication

If they're hard to reach or don't explain things clearly during the sales process, that won't improve after you pay. Communication is everything.

What Makes a Great Edmonton Web Designer

When you find the right fit, you're looking for someone who:

  • Listens to your goals and understands your business
  • Builds with results in mind, not just aesthetics
  • Has proven experience with small businesses like yours
  • Explains things clearly and answers questions patiently
  • Handles technical SEO and local search
  • Provides ongoing support and maintenance
  • Is transparent about costs with no hidden fees
  • Takes pride in their work and your success
